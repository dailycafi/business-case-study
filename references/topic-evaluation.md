# Topic Evaluation Methodology

## Core Philosophy

**Topic selection is about finding blanks, not filling slots.**

Teaching value formula:
```
Value = f(Importance × Discussion Space) × Novelty Coefficient
```

Novelty is a **multiplier**, not an addend. A highly important topic with zero novelty (written hundreds of times) has near-zero marginal teaching value. A moderately important topic in blank territory can be transformative.

---

## Three-Line Parallel Research

### Line A: Company Deep Scan
- Founder/core management background, founding story, key turning points
- Development timeline and milestones
- Financial data and trends (revenue, profit, gross margin, R&D investment)
- Core technology/products/business model
- Customer structure and concentration
- Key strategic decisions (made and facing)
- Organizational structure and governance
- Recent major events
- Search in both Chinese and English: prospectus, annual reports, analyst reports, media coverage

### Line B: Industry Panorama Analysis
- Industry definition and technology route classification
- Global and China market size and growth trends
- Supply chain structure (upstream, midstream, downstream)
- Competitive landscape: detailed competitor profiles (tech routes, scale, customers, financing, recent developments)
- Downstream application market analysis (segment size, growth rate, penetration)
- Industry barriers analysis
- Driving factors and key trends
- Major industry events (M&A, tech breakthroughs, policy changes)

### Line C: Topic Novelty Assessment

**Core task — existing case search:**
Search these case libraries systematically:
- Harvard Business Publishing
- Case Centre
- CEIBS (中欧), Tsinghua (清华), Peking (北大), CKGSB (长江) and other domestic case libraries

For each search, determine:
1. Does this company already have cases? What angles? (Must avoid duplication)
2. What cases exist for same-industry/same-type companies?
3. How saturated is the same strategic topic (e.g., "digital transformation", "going global", "platformization")? Written hundreds of times, or relatively blank?

**Company uniqueness identification:** Based on Lines A and B, identify what distinguishes this company from peers.

**Multi-direction divergence:** Propose 5–7 potential case directions without pre-constraining to a single course boundary.

## Five-Dimension Evaluation Matrix

For each potential direction, systematically evaluate:

**Dimension weights (for prioritization):**

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Novelty | Highest (最高) | Multiplier effect — zero novelty kills value |
| Teaching Value | High (高) | Core purpose of a teaching case |
| Theoretical Richness | Medium-High (中高) | Enables depth and elevation |
| Information Availability | Medium (中) | Practical constraint |
| Discussion Scope | Medium (中) | Structural fit |

### Dimension 1: Novelty (Highest Weight)
- Has this angle been sufficiently covered in existing case libraries?
- Does it provide a completely new discussion dimension or analytical perspective?
- Can it form clear differentiation from existing cases on the same company (if any)?

Rating scale:
- ★☆☆☆☆ Written hundreds of times already
- ★★★★★ Nearly blank territory

### Dimension 2: Teaching Value
- Does it involve problems students will likely encounter in their future careers?
- Is there sufficient decision tension (no obvious "correct answer")?
- Can it spark collision of different viewpoints?

Rating scale:
- ★☆☆☆☆ No discussion space
- ★★★★★ Naturally controversial

### Dimension 3: Theoretical Richness
- Are there 2–3 mature theoretical frameworks that can be connected?
- Is there space for theoretical integration or extension ("升维")?
- Is the theoretical perspective novel (new application of classic theory > repetitive use of old frameworks)?

Rating scale:
- ★☆☆☆☆ Weak theoretical support
- ★★★★★ Multi-theory intersection with elevation potential

### Dimension 4: Information Availability
- Primary cases: Can key information be obtained through interviews? Is the company willing to cooperate?
- Secondary cases: Is public information sufficient? Is there high-quality data support?
- What are "fatal gaps" (without which no good case can be written)?

Rating scale:
- ★☆☆☆☆ Key information unobtainable
- ★★★★★ Rich and accessible information

### Dimension 5: Discussion Scope
- Can the topic be fully developed within 4–6 discussion questions?
- Is it too narrow (done in 2 questions) or too broad (needs 10 questions)?
- Is it better as a standalone case or as a sub-topic of another direction?

Rating scale:
- ★☆☆☆☆ Too narrow or too broad
- ★★★★★ Exactly 4–6 questions

### Tier Classification
- **Tier 1 (Strongly recommended):** Multiple dimensions ★★★★+, novelty must be ★★★★+
- **Tier 2 (Optional):** Generally good but one dimension weak, or better as sub-topic
- **Tier 3 (Not recommended):** Insufficient novelty or information unavailable

## Direction Integration and Decision Point Design

Single directions may be too narrow; multiple directions may be too scattered. Find the optimal integration:

### Consider Direction Merging
Two Tier 1 directions may integrate into one richer case. Example: "technology platformization" + "growth ceiling" can be connected through a single decision scenario.

### Main Line + Sub-topics
One direction as the main line, others embedded as analytical dimensions.

### Design the Core Decision Point
A good case needs a clear decision node — the protagonist faces a choice at a specific time, and the choice is genuinely difficult. The decision point should:
- Have real time pressure or urgency
- Integrate multiple analytical dimensions
- Have no obvious "correct answer"
- Differentiate from existing cases on the same company along these dimensions:

**Differentiation dimensions table:**

| Dimension | Pole A | Pole B |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Time perspective | Retrospective (回顾性) | Forward-looking (前瞻性) |
| Analysis level | Strategic (战略层) | Execution (执行层) |
| Subject perspective | Whole enterprise | Business line / Individual manager |
| Course positioning | Strategy / Organization | Marketing / Operations / Finance |

## Theoretical Pre-assessment

For the final 2–3 recommended directions, pre-assess 2–3 potentially fitting theoretical frameworks each:
- Core argument of the theory
- Why it may apply to this case
- Teaching value and novelty
- Potential for theoretical integration or elevation

---

## Interview Outline Design Principles (for Primary Cases)

1. **Organize by thematic modules**, not chronologically
2. **Annotate the purpose** of each question (which discussion Q it serves)
3. **Separate outlines per direction** — different recommended directions get independent outlines for flexible combination
4. **Flow from rapport to core to close** — warm-up questions → core strategic questions → wrap-up/future
5. **Ask for specifics, not abstractions** — "Can you walk me through a specific example of how that decision was made?" is 100x more valuable than "What's your view on innovation?"
6. **Pre-embed follow-up triggers** — design questions that naturally lead to follow-ups (e.g., "You mentioned X earlier — how did the team react?")
7. **Quantity control**: aim for 20–30 questions, 60–90 minutes total interview time
8. Include "fatal gap" questions — information without which the case cannot work

**Interview technique reminders** (attach to outline for interviewer):
- Capture internal disagreements — ask "Was everyone on the same page?" or "Who pushed back?"
- Pin down key numbers — revenue, headcount, timeline, market share
- Watch for emotional moments — pauses, tone shifts, hedging — these often signal the real story

---

## Topic Self-Check Checklist

Before finalizing topic direction:
- [ ] Novelty confirmed: no existing case covers this exact angle
- [ ] Decision point is clear: who, when, what choice, why difficult
- [ ] 4–6 discussion questions can be naturally designed
- [ ] 2–3 theoretical frameworks identified with integration potential
- [ ] Key information is available or obtainable
- [ ] Differentiated from existing cases on same company (if any)
- [ ] Relevant to target course and student audience
