# Case Writing Style Guide

## Core Principle: Narrative Style (HBS + WSJ)

Teaching cases are stories, not reports. Write in narrative style with specific people, times, places, dialogue, and dramatic tension. The reader should feel they are witnessing events unfold.

## Development Order: Teaching Note First

**Common mistake:** Write complete case body first, then add teaching note.
**Correct approach:** Design learning objectives and discussion questions first, then write body guided by these.

```
Step 1: Determine learning objectives (4–6)
Step 2: Design discussion questions (4–6, matching objectives)
Step 3: Reverse-engineer what information the body needs
Step 4: Write case body
Step 5: Complete teaching note
```

## "No Redundancy, No Gaps" Principle (Flexible Application)

This is a guiding principle, not a mechanical rule. Narrative completeness and flow always take priority.

**No Redundancy:** Main body content should serve discussion questions. If a section doesn't support any question, consider trimming. But "background" content needed for narrative completeness is acceptable.

**No Gaps:** Students should find sufficient information in the body to answer each question. Key decision context, constraints, and stakeholders cannot be missing. Exhibits supplement the body.

**Information coverage check (reference tool, not mandatory):**
```
         Q1    Q2    Q3    Q4    Q5
Sec 1    ✓✓    ✓     ~     -     -
Sec 2    ✓     ✓✓    ✓     ~     -
Sec 3    -     ✓     ✓✓    ✓     -
Sec 4    ~     -     ✓     ✓✓    ✓
Sec 5    -     -     -     ✓     ✓✓

✓✓ = core supporting info (must have)
✓  = supplementary info
~  = background/narrative context
-  = not directly related

Flexible checks:
1. Each Q has at least one "✓✓" section
2. If a section is all "-", consider if needed (don't force removal)
3. "~" marks are normal — narrative needs
4. Prioritize story completeness and flow over precise mapping
```

**Practical advice:**
- First draft: write naturally following story logic, don't be constrained
- Revision: check that key discussion points have sufficient info
- Final: trim obvious redundancy without hurting reading experience

## Writing Techniques

### Technique 1: Specific Details Over Abstract Descriptions

**Pre-check:** Before making content vivid, verify it supports a discussion question. If it doesn't support any, consider removing.

```
BAD abstract: "The company faced severe market challenges"
GOOD specific: "In Q3 2023, of Pukang's 5 core brands, 3 demanded
               a 20% reduction in service fees"
```

### Technique 2: Dialogue and Scene for Visual Impact
```
BAD flat: "The management team disagreed on AI strategy"
GOOD vivid:
"CMO Qin Ziyi raised a challenge: 'AI is an enablement tool,
not the core driver.' CTO Wang Yong immediately responded:
'Exactly why we should externalize it — build a platform.'
The meeting room fell into brief silence."
```

### Technique 3: Data Contrasts for Tension
```
BAD monotone: "Company performance grew"
GOOD tension: "Revenue went from 1.8B in 2014 to 8B in 2022,
              growing 344%. But 2023 growth plummeted to 15% —
              the lowest since founding."
```

### Technique 4: Suspense and Turning Points
```
Example (CR Snow Beer):
"As the leader of the beer company with the largest market share,
Hou Xiaohai's mood during this visit was far from relaxed."
→ Reader wonders: Why not relaxed? Even the market leader has worries?
Then gradually reveal: premiumization dilemma, rising competitors, industry ceiling...
```

### Technique 5: Time Anchors at Key Nodes
```
"In 2018, Pukang launched digital transformation..."
"However, by 2020, Yu Yong realized..."
"The turning point came in 2022..."

Effects:
- Helps readers build timeline
- Makes causal relationships clearer
- Strengthens narrative rhythm
```

## Strictly Forbidden Expressions

| Type | Forbidden Examples | Correct Alternative |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Media sensationalism | "横空出世" "震惊业界" "颠覆传统" | Objectively describe data and market reactions |
| Consulting jargon | "赋能用户" "抓手" "闭环" "组合拳" "打法" | Use formal academic/business equivalents |
| Ad hyperbole | "行业领先" "国际一流" "顶尖技术" | Support with specific data and rankings |
| Internet slang | "破防了" "打工人" "内卷" "躺平" | Use formal business language |
| Subjective judgment | "明智的决策" "出色的领导力" | Describe process and results; let readers judge |

## Narrative Structure

### Default: Decision Frame Cut (三段式)

This is the standard structure. Use it unless there is a specific reason not to.

```
Part 1: Decision Scene (10–15% of total)
├─ Opening: specific time/place + key person + core decision
├─ Conflict: clashing viewpoints (through dialogue)
└─ Suspense: complexity and urgency of the problem

Part 2: Background and Development (70–80%)
├─ Industry background (5–10%): trends, competition, policy
├─ Company history (15–20%): milestones, key nodes, strategic evolution
├─ Core issues (40–50%): deep analysis, multi-dimensional information
└─ Key data: woven into narrative, supplemented by Exhibits

Part 3: Return to Decision (5–10%)
├─ Restate the decision dilemma
├─ Emphasize importance and complexity
└─ Open ending — leave space for student thinking
```

### Alternative: Story Line Cut (chronological)

Rarely needed, but appropriate when the case's teaching value comes from the journey itself rather than a single decision point — e.g., a multi-decade transformation where the pattern of decisions matters more than any one choice. In this structure, events unfold chronologically, with the decision point emerging at the end. Only use if the user explicitly requests it or if the Decision Frame Cut clearly does not fit.

## Opening Design Examples

### Chinese (CR Snow Beer):
"2024年7月6日，华润啤酒董事会主席侯孝海受邀出席了'中国啤酒T5峰会'。作为行业T5中市场份额最大的啤酒企业掌门人，侯孝海此行的心情并不轻松..."

Features: specific date, specific event, key person, emotional setup, suspense

### English (POOK):
"In the boardroom of Shanghai POOK Data Technology (Group) Co., Ltd., founder Yu Yong and his core executive team were discussing the company's future, with Artificial Intelligence (AI) as the discussion's central theme. 'We need to decide,' Yu Yong said after a pause, 'whether to keep our AI technology exclusive for a competitive edge or turn it into a product, perhaps even making it part of industry infrastructure.'"

Features: direct scene entry, dialogue presents core decision, concise, grabs attention

## Exhibits Design

- Quantity: 5–15
- Types: timeline, org chart, financial tables, business process diagrams, competitive landscape comparison, strategy framework illustrations
- Each must have:
  - Number: Exhibit 1, Exhibit 2...
  - Clear title
  - Source: "Source: Provided by [Company]" or "Source: [Publication], [Date]"

## Citation Format

**In-body hyperlinks:**
```
根据麦肯锡报告，到2024年全球72%的组织采用了生成式AI[1]。
```

**APA endnotes (at case end):**
```
1 Alex Singla et al. The state of AI in early 2024. May 30, 2024.
  https://www.mckinsey.com/...

2 周嘉宝. 美妆巨头也要降本增效. 时代财经, 2024-09-24[2024-10-19].
  https://www.tfcaijing.com/...
```

## Word Count Standards
- Chinese cases: ≤10,000 characters, body ≤12 pages (including appendix)
- English cases: ≤6,000 words, body ≤15 pages (including appendix)
