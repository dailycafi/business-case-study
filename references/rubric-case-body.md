# Case Body Assessment Rubric (100 Points)

Based on case award evaluation criteria + award-winning case analysis.

## Dimension 1: Importance and Decision Focus (25 Points)

**Award criterion:** Case focuses on urgent, frontier management issues in real business environments.

### Excellent (23–25)
- Describes a typical challenge, development dilemma, key decision, or important issue
- Clear "discussion focus" requiring analytical methods, tools, concepts, frameworks, or theories
- Adapted to Chinese business education context, reflecting Chinese enterprise practices
- Focuses on forward-looking, pioneering management issues not yet widely discussed

**Decision scene setup template:**
1. Specific time/place + key person + important meeting/event
2. Core decision dilemma (through dialogue or internal monologue)
3. Brief background
4. Return to decision focus

**Example (CR Snow Beer):**
"On July 6, 2024, Hou Xiaohai, Chairman of CR Beer, attended the 'China Beer T5 Summit'. As the leader of the T5 company with the largest market share, Hou's mood was far from relaxed..."

**Decision focus types:**
- Strategic direction choice: "Maintain AI technology internally for competitive advantage, or externalize it as industry infrastructure?"
- Business model transformation: from service provider to ecosystem platform
- Technology investment decision: whether to invest massively in new technology

**Management dilemma elements:**
- Conflict: different viewpoints clashing within team
- Urgency: external pressure (economic downturn, client cost-cutting)
- Complexity: multiple stakeholders, no obvious "correct answer"

### Good (20–22)
- Focus basically clear but decision urgency insufficient
- Issue somewhat important but not frontier enough
- Chinese context not fully reflected
- Scene setup flat, lacking dramatic tension

### Passing (15–19)
- Focus blurry, decision point unclear
- Issue relatively conventional, lacking novelty
- Low relevance to Chinese management education
- Opening too straightforward to attract readers

### Needs Improvement (0–14)
- No clear decision focus or management dilemma
- Outdated or unimportant issue
- Unsuitable for Chinese business education

## Dimension 2: Complexity and Controversy (25 Points)

**Award criterion:** Discussion focus has complexity (not surface-level) and controversy (thinking space for students).

### Excellent (23–25)
- Complexity: focus goes beyond surface description to deep structural issues
- Controversy: sufficient space for students to debate from different perspectives
- Multi-dimensional: information supports analysis from multiple angles (financial, strategic, organizational, ethical)

**Information layer design:**
```
Layer 1: Surface phenomena
- Direct problems (sales decline, intensified competition)

Layer 2: Deep contradictions
- Strategic positioning conflicts
- Organizational capability mismatches
- Business model dilemmas

Layer 3: Essential challenges
- Industry rule changes
- Value creation logic evolution
- Ecosystem restructuring
```

**Controversy point setup (POOK case example):**
```
CMO view: "AI is not our core growth driver, but an enablement tool"
  vs.
CTO view: "We should externalize technology, build industry infrastructure"
  vs.
Other executives: "Will we lose focus? Our success is in consumer insights"
```

**Multi-dimensional analysis space (Pukang case):**
- Strategic: from offline distribution to digital ecosystem service provider
- Organizational: Amoeba → Amoeba+Middle platform → Platform ecosystem
- Capability: Dynamic capabilities (Sensing-Seizing-Transforming)
- Competitive: from dynamic capabilities to data network effects

### Good (20–22): Some complexity but layers unclear; controversy exists but opposing views not fully presented
### Passing (15–19): Insufficient complexity; weak controversy; limited dimensions
### Needs Improvement (0–14): Too simple; no debate space; information doesn't support multi-dimensional analysis

## Dimension 3: Information Rigor and Sufficiency (25 Points)

**Award criterion:** Provides fact-based information from reliable sources with proper citations.

### Excellent (23–25)
- Reliable sources with hyperlinks and APA endnotes
- Sufficient but not redundant information with appropriate tension
- Key data verified: time, numbers, proper nouns accurate
- All facts have source attribution

**Citation format — hyperlinks in body:**
```
According to McKinsey, by 2024, 72% of organizations globally adopted GenAI
in at least one business function[1].
```

**APA endnotes at case end:**
```
1 Alex Singla et al. The state of AI in early 2024. May 30, 2024.
  https://www.mckinsey.com/...
```

**Source priority (high to low):**
1. Primary: company interviews, internal documents (authorized), field research
2. Authoritative secondary: academic journals (SSCI/SCI), official statistics, mainstream financial media (WSJ, FT, Caijing), annual reports, prospectuses
3. Not acceptable: self-media, marketing puff pieces, unverified online information

**Information density standard:**
- Chinese cases: ~60–70 key information points per 10,000 characters
- English cases: ~50–60 key information points per 10,000 words
- Exhibits: 5–15 charts, each with clear teaching purpose

**Data completeness checklist:**
- [ ] Every key data point has clear source
- [ ] Hyperlinks are functional (clickable)
- [ ] APA format complete (author, title, date, URL)
- [ ] Time ranges explicit ("Q2 2024" not "recently")
- [ ] Units accurate ("$137M" not "about $137M")
- [ ] Proper nouns spelled correctly

**Required information categories:**
- [ ] Background: industry overview, competitive landscape, policy environment
- [ ] Company: history, business structure, financials, org chart
- [ ] Decision context: problem background, stakeholders, options, constraints
- [ ] Supporting detail: specific events, key dialogues, internal documents

### Good (20–22): Most info sourced, minor gaps; format mostly correct; basically sufficient
### Passing (15–19): Some key info unsourced; format inconsistent; density issues
### Needs Improvement (0–14): Widespread missing sources; format chaotic; insufficient for discussion

## Dimension 4: Writing Quality and Readability (25 Points)

**Award criterion:** Well-structured, logically clear, concise language, strong readability.

### Excellent (23–25)
- Well-structured with clear logic and concise language
- Word count: Chinese ≤10,000 words (body ≤12 pages with appendix); English ≤6,000 words (≤15 pages)
- Objective and neutral: no subjective descriptions guiding students to "correct" answers
- Full narrative style (HBS + WSJ), compelling storytelling

**Standard three-part structure:**
```
Part 1: Decision Scene (10–15%)
├─ Opening: specific time/place + key person + core decision
├─ Conflict: clashing viewpoints (through dialogue)
└─ Suspense: complexity and urgency

Part 2: Background and Development (70–80%)
├─ Industry background (5–10%)
├─ Company history (15–20%)
├─ Core issues (40–50%): deep analysis, multi-dimensional info
└─ Key data: woven into narrative, supplemented by Exhibits

Part 3: Return to Decision (5–10%)
├─ Restate decision dilemma
├─ Emphasize importance and complexity
└─ Open ending for student reflection
```

**Strictly forbidden expressions:**

| Type | Forbidden | Correct |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Sensationalism | "横空出世" "震惊业界" "颠覆传统" | Objectively describe growth data and market reactions |
| Consulting jargon | "赋能" "抓手" "闭环" "组合拳" "打法" | Use formal academic/business language |
| Ad hyperbole | "行业领先" "国际一流" "顶尖技术" | Support with specific data and rankings |
| Internet slang | "破防" "打工人" "内卷" "躺平" | Use formal business language |
| Subjective judgment | "明智的决策" "出色的领导力" | Describe process and results, let readers judge |

**Chinese vs. English writing differences:**

| Aspect | Chinese | English |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Opening | Can set background (100–150 chars) | More direct scene entry (50–80 words) |
| Dialogue | Moderate use, Chinese expression habits | More frequent, natural viewpoint conflict |
| Paragraph length | 150–250 chars | 100–150 words |
| Data presentation | Prefer tables | Text + charts combined |

**Exhibits design:**
- Quantity: 5–15
- Types: timeline, org chart, financial tables, process diagrams, competitive landscape, strategy frameworks
- Each must have: number (Exhibit 1, 2...), clear title, source (Source: ...)

### Good (20–22): Basically structured; mostly objective; somewhat narrative but not vivid
### Passing (15–19): Loose structure; insufficient objectivity; weak narrative; report-like
### Needs Improvement (0–14): Chaotic structure; heavy subjective judgment; no narrative quality
